Definition

What is an automation audit?

An automation audit answers three questions about your business with evidence instead of opinions: where your team's hours actually go, which of that work a machine could do reliably, and what each fix is worth in saved hours and dollars. The output is a written roadmap, not a software demo.

It is different from hiring a consultancy (open-ended meter, long discovery) and different from a vendor pitch (the answer is always their platform). The audit is fixed-fee, fixed-timeline, and tool-neutral: recommendations build into the systems you already run, like your existing ERP, ticketing, and Microsoft 365 stack.

If there is not much worth automating, the audit says so. That honesty is the product.

Timing

When does your team need one?

You do not need a transformation program. You need an audit when several of these sound familiar:

  • People retype data from PDFs and emails into your systems every week
  • The same report gets rebuilt by hand every week or month
  • Approvals crawl across inboxes and spreadsheets with no owner
  • Headcount in admin roles grows with revenue instead of staying flat
  • Leadership keeps saying "we should be using AI" but nobody can say where

Each of those is a measurable cost. The audit counts it before you spend anything fixing it.

Questions

Common questions about the audit.

How long does an automation audit take?

About two weeks. Week one is a few working sessions to see the real workflows. Week two you get the written roadmap and a live readout call. Your team's total time commitment is a couple of hours.

What access do you need from us?

A couple of hours of your team's time across a week, and a look at the workflows and systems involved, usually over screen shares. Access is read-only and nothing is automated or changed during the audit. It is diagnosis first.

Is the audit just a pitch to buy automation work?

No. The roadmap is yours to act on with anyone, including your own team. If you do build with KHIPUAI within 30 days, the full audit fee credits toward the build. And if there is not much worth automating, the audit says so plainly.

What kinds of work usually get automated?

The recurring offenders are document intake and data entry, hand-built reports, reconciliations between systems that do not talk, approval chasing across inboxes, and copying the same data into multiple tools. The audit puts hours and dollars on each one before recommending anything.

Do you guarantee the savings numbers?

No, and you should be wary of anyone who does. The audit uses conservative estimates built from your team's actual volumes and time, so you can judge each opportunity on evidence. Results described on this site are one firm's outcome, not a typical or guaranteed result.

Can the audit be done remotely?

Yes. The audit runs fully remote over video calls and screen shares, on US time zones. On-site can be arranged for Plus engagements when it genuinely helps.
